Te Whare Tapa Whā

Here is my wellbeing plan, following Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā model:

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

  • Meal prep each weekend
  • Do lunchtime runs
  • Follow a workout video each evening
  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep each night
  • Drink at least 2L each day

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

  • Be proactive in making plans with my friends, so I always know when I’ll see them next
  • Spend time with my family each day
  • Support my sister with her newborn

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

  • Follow a daily routine to help maintain habits that will improve my wellbeing
  • Make time for reflection each day so I can assess my wellbeing, and adjust things if needed
  • Use the Balance app to practice mindfulness
  • Incorporate regular breaks into my study

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

  • Set aside time each morning for God, to pray and read the Bible
  • Read morning and evening devotionals
  • Journal each night

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

  • Walk around the block each morning to connect with the day
  • On clear nights, spend time looking at the stars
