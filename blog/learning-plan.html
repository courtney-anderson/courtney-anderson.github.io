Learning Plan

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

Because I’m certain now that I’m returning to my current workplace after this course, where I’ll be a UX Designer for at least another six months before potentially transitioning to a junior developer role, I want to become confident in the things that will enhance my job and the way I work with developers (e.g. component libraries).

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strength is my existing experience working in a digital team; I’ve worked alongside developers and am good at collaboration & meetings, and I also have the design skills if mock-ups are needed for any projects.

My main limitation is still sticking to a timeframe, but I think working with others will resolve this, as the expectations of others definitely keep me in line!

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

My biggest non-technical challenge will be staying focused for nine weeks, and pushing forward when I feel stuck. I want to manage my time well so that I learn as much as possible while also maintaining my wellbeing.

What non-technical skills (human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I want to eliminate my fixed mindset if possible! And be as confident over chat as I would be in person.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Still the same as Foundations: to provide relevant guidance when needed.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I want to go into each week prepared, so I’ll set aside time to do this, either on Fridays or in the weekend. I also want to stick to a daily routine to maintain my wellbeing, and the Bootcamp schedule.

I will reflect often in order to identify any gaps, and reach out to others when I need support. I will also keep my end goals in mind, to motivate and guide me throughout the rest of the course.

Foundations Learning Plan

What is my long term goal or career pathway?

I'm mainly using this course to determine if I'd prefer the development side of websites rather than the design side (I'm currently a user experience designer that mocks up high-fidelity designs for developers to implement). Currently I see four potential outcomes:

  1. I realise I still prefer the design-side, but use my knowledge of code to improve hand-off with the developers I work with, and also to inform my work on design systems.
  2. I realise I prefer coding, and transition into a developer role at my current work.
  3. I realise I prefer coding, but a specific subset, so I look specifically for a developer role that allows me to further upskill in the areas I enjoy.
  4. I realise I enjoy design and coding equally, so potentially work on side-projects that cover both skills (e.g. app building) until I can transition this into a full-time gig.

What are my strengths and limitations when it comes to learning?

Creating this website definitely highlighted some strengths and limitations I have when it comes to learning: mainly that I can come up with the concept I want to create fairly quickly but, if this takes longer than the recommended time to implement, I find it extremely hard to adjust the vision I have (very all-or-nothing!). So to overcome this, I want to be more diligent in sticking to the suggested timeframes by splitting each timeframe into two, and using that halfway point to reassess and adjust my gameplan when needed.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would I like to see developed in myself while at Dev Academy?

I want to be a lot more proactive, both in socialising with others in the chat & in-person, as well as proactively upskilling myself. Daily reflection will help with this, as I can identify the specific ways I can be more proactive.

How will I manage my workload in this programme?

I want to stick to a daily routine so that I have a good work/life balance, as by improving my wellbeing I expect my ability to manage the workload will also improve. Additionally I want to stick to the suggested timeframes and also look ahead to what's coming, as I so far I've tended to spend too much time on certain tasks at the expense of other priorities.

How and where will I seek help in a timely way?

When I get stuck, this is current steps I go through until I'm unstuck:

  1. Ask Google.
  2. Ask ChatGPT.
  3. Ask my family (if non-technical).
  4. Ask others from my homegroup.
  5. Ask in the relevant Dev Academy Discord forum.

What do I expect from the Facilitation team?

Relevant guidance when needed :)

How have I scheduled my study time vs blocked-out time?

I've used my Google Calandar to timeblock out each day, ensuring I'm getting in enough study time as well as maintaining my wellbeing.
